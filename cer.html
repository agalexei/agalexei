Clean Energy Regulator

The Clean Energy Regulator is a national committee that helps administer government legislation to manage and offset carbon emissions.

Client
Government Agency
Year
2020
Service
Web Redesign
Website
cleanenergyregulator.gov.au
Part 1

The Problem

Users feel that Government websites like the CER are stale, are hard to navigate and have too much, small text.

The Solution

The CER website was redesigned to make the navigation user friendly, and reduce the information overload.

Part 4

Heirustic Evaluation

Appearance

The CER website, though had a consistent text and colour design, could be redone. We also found that white space wasn’t used effectively and that the primary goal of the website was not clear.


Content

The CER website had too much text on the site, it was not easily scanned and headings were confusing.


Navigation

Navigation on the CER website can be improved by limiting the number of buttons and links. Information could also be reorganised to make sense.


Card Sorting

Approaching the card sorting, we understood that the top navigation bar was lacking in functionality.


Simplifying the top bar navigation would make finding the information more functional and easy.

We also wanted to make finding the login button and links to social media accounts accessible on each page.

Part 5

Wireframes

Our wireframe redesigned the homepage to have a card system.

We also incorporated the subscribe container at the bottom of the page.

Through our research, we found users instinctively scrolled to the bottom of the page to subscribe.

Secondary Wireframes

Headers were again used to clearly highlight which page the user had landed on.

Text is minimal so that the user does not feel overwhelmed.

We also incorporated the subscribe container at the bottom of the page.

Mobile Site Wireframe

The mobile site for the CER was not effectively laidout. The burger menu appeared on the left side, which we have moved to the right.

We also incorporated a secondary page specifically for the drop down menu.

TOur goal with the mobile site was to make the user’s experience feel less cluttered and still know how to navigate through the vast content.

Part 6

Prototype Testing

Through user testing, we uncovered slight changes that needed to be made such as labelling the “login” button in the burger menu. Our testing was focussed on making the use of the site seamless and intuitive.

Part 7

Conclusion

We had created a new CER desktop and mobile site that was more seamless for the user to use and navigate. Features of the site and its uses were better outlined so that users did not feel lost navigating the site. We had also improved the subscription functionality as well as registration for events.

Future implementations for the CER would be breadcrumbs on pages to help with navigation, iterating the sign-in process and further iterations on the mobile site.
